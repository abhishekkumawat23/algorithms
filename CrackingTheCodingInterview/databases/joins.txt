Question:
What are the different types of joins?
Please explain how they differ and why certain types are better in certain situations?

Answer:
1. Joins are used to combine the results of two tables.
2. To perform a join, each of the tables must have atleast one field that will be used to find matching records from the other table.
3. Inner join: Result set contains data where the criteria match.
4. Outer Join: Outer join will have inner join data and may also contain some records that have no matching record in the other table.
   a. Left join: Result will contain all results from left table.
   b. Right join: Result will contain all results from right table.
   c. Full join: Result will contain results from both table.
